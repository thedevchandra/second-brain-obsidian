# Systems & Tools

## Standard of Excellence
Maintain a lean, integrated system where Obsidian handles thinking/knowledge and Notion handles databases/project management. Clear boundaries, minimal duplication.

## System Architecture

### Obsidian Second Brain (This Vault)
**Purpose:** Thinking, learning, knowledge, personal reflection

**What lives here:**
- Daily notes and journal entries
- Meeting notes with people
- Book notes and article clippings
- Research and learning notes
- Evergreen notes (concepts, principles)
- Areas of responsibility
- Small projects with mostly notes
- Personal reflections and thinking

### Notion Workspace
**Purpose:** Databases, structured project management, tracking

**What lives there:**
- Project management databases
- Reading lists and book database
- Habit trackers
- CRM / People database
- Goal tracking with progress charts
- Team collaboration spaces
- Complex projects with multiple people

## Notes

The goal is NOT to replace Notion entirely. Both tools are powerful. The goal is to use each for what it does best:
- **Notion** = Structure, databases, collaboration, tracking
- **Obsidian** = Thinking, connections, knowledge, reflection

Your thinking should flow. Your tracking should be structured. Use both.
