**Unit Discipline:**

- NJP Authority:
    - COs (Commanding Officers) of Commissioned units hold Non-Judicial Punishment (NJP) authority for personnel assigned to their respective units.
    - NRAU (Navy Reserve Activity Unit) COs have limited NJP authority, typically extending to administrative actions and recommendations.
    - Other Discipline Types: Besides NJP, other disciplinary measures available include counseling, reassignment, administrative separation, and referral to higher authority for disciplinary action.
- **UCMJ Jurisdiction:** SELRES are subject to the UCMJ under various orders with certain exceptions: IDT, IDTT, AT, ADT, ADOS, ATP, and RMP.
- **Administrative Separation Board:** Evaluate whether a member should be retained in the Navy Reserve or separated from service.
    - The process involves notification of charges, convening of the board, presentation of evidence, and a decision rendered by the board.
    - The member is afforded due process rights throughout the proceedings.
- **Authorities and UCMJ Violations:**
    - XO: Discipline unit members through counseling, corrective action, and recommendation for NJP.
        - In case of a UCMJ violation, resources include legal counsel, Judge Advocate General (JAG) support, and the convening authority for disciplinary proceedings.
    - CO: The commanding officer of the respective command will prosecute the offense.
        - Legal counsel will advocate for the offending Sailor, ensuring their rights are protected.
        - The offending Sailor has the right to a fair trial, legal representation, and the responsibility to cooperate with the legal process.

**Unit-wide Uniform Inspection:**

- Conduct a unit-wide uniform inspection to ensure compliance with uniform and grooming standards.
- Provide clear guidance on uniform regulations and grooming standards.
- Emphasize the importance of professional appearance and adherence to Navy standards.
- Conduct inspections regularly to maintain standards and address any deficiencies promptly.
