# Triad

In the Navy Reserve, the unit triad refers to the three key leadership positions within a Reserve unit. These positions are critical for the effective management, leadership, and operational readiness of the unit. The unit triad consists of:

## **Commanding Officer (CO)**

- Responsible for the overall command, leadership, and management of the unit.
- Sets the vision, establishes priorities, and ensures the unit meets its mission requirements.
- Are ultimately accountable for all aspects of the unit's performance and welfare of its personnel.

## **Executive Officer (XO)**

- Assists the Commanding Officer in the day-to-day operations of the unit.
- Acts as the second-in-command and is responsible for implementing the CO's policies and directives.
- Oversee administrative functions, training programs, and ensure the unit maintains its readiness standards.

## **Command Master Chief (CMC)**

- Senior enlisted advisor to the CO and XO.
- Represents the enlisted personnel and provides insight and recommendations on issues affecting the enlisted ranks.
- Play a crucial role in maintaining morale, discipline, and professional development within the unit.
- Ensures effective communication between the enlisted personnel and the unit's leadership.

Together, the unit triad works collaboratively to ensure the unit operates smoothly, maintains readiness, and achieves its mission objectives.

# Administrative Officer

**Personally Identifiable Information (PII):**

- Defined as any information that can identify an individual.
- Policies governing PII use are crucial for privacy protection, trust maintenance, and legal compliance.
- Breaches should be reported immediately to supervisors and designated officials, then to higher authorities for appropriate action.

**Unit Communication:**

- Effective communication is vital for command cohesion and readiness.
- Each tool facilitates communication and information dissemination. serves a specific purpose, and must be used responsibly:
    - **Plan of the Month (POM)**: Communicates upcoming events, duties, and expectations to the command. It allows everyone to plan ahead and be prepared for what's coming.
    - **Recall Bill**: It's essential in case of emergencies or sudden changes in plans. A well-maintained recall bill ensures that all personnel can be reached quickly and efficiently.

**Navy Reserve Readiness Module (NRRM):**

- NRRM aids in managing and tracking unit readiness.
- The Command Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of readiness indicators.
    - **Billets**: Manage billet assignments, ensuring personnel are correctly assigned and that all billets are filled.
    - **Fit**: Shows training status and qualifications to ensure everyone is meeting their training requirements.
    - **Individual Medical Readiness (IMR)**: Track the medical readiness of your personnel, including immunizations, dental status, and periodic health assessments.
    - **Deployability**: Provides a snapshot of your unit's overall readiness to deploy, taking into account various factors such as personnel availability, medical readiness, and training status.
    - **Smart Reserve Unit Assignment Document (RUAD)**: Provides a detailed view of your unit's manning, including billets and assignments, and can be used to manage assignments and track manning levels.

**Drill Policy:**

- **Satisfactory Participation or Year ([BUPERSINST 1001.39F](https://www.mynavyhr.navy.mil/Portals/55/Reference/Instructions/BUPERS/1001.39_CH-1.pdf); Pg 11-4 or MILPERSMAN 1001-150)** requires meeting specific requirements over the fiscal year:
    - Perform minimum of 40 drill periods and 12 days AT
    - Less than 9 UA per rolling 12-month period
    - Members must report as directed for physical and dental examinations and provide requested medical information for retention in the Navy Reserve
    - Keep CO informed of matters affecting mobilization readiness
    - Respond to official correspondence
    - Must comply with an involuntary recall to Active Duty
    - Mandatory as unauthorized absences or missed AT can result in AdSep
    - **Fiscal Year**: The government's budget year, running from October 1 to September 30.
- **Good Year or Qualifying Year (MILPERSMAN 1820-050)**: A year when a reservist earns a minimum of 50 retirement points within their anniversary year:
    - 20 Qualifying Years to be eligible for Retirement
    - **Anniversary Year**: A 12-month period starting on the date the service member joined the Reserve.
- Different drill situations:
    - **Local Drill**: Standard drill done at a local training site for regular training.
    - **Remote Drill**: Drill performed outside of a local training site (telework). It's suitable when a member cannot physically be present due to various circumstances. Must have telework agreement on file
    - **Voluntary Drilling Beyond Local Drill Distance**: Drill at a site beyond their normal commuting distance. The member is responsible for their own travel and accommodations.
    - **Flex Drill**: Drill performed outside of the normal drill weekend for members who cannot attend the regular drill due to work or personal reasons.
    - **Inactive Duty for Training (IDT)**: normal drill and may be completed locally, remotely, or be rescheduled.
    - **Additional Training Periods (ATPs)**: Additional paid IDT periods for specific units/billets to support training or a unit's specified mobilization mission.
    - **Readiness Management Periods (RMPs)**: Additional paid IDT used to support day-to-day unit operations and to accomplish unit administration, training preparation, support activities, and maintenance functions.

**Manpower:**

- SMART RUAD is crucial for personnel management:
    - **MAS Code**: The Manning Authorization Strength (MAS) code provides details about a unit's authorized strength in terms of personnel, especially in assessing if the unit is undermanned or overmanned.
    - **RFAS Code**: The Reserve Force Activity Status (RFAS) code indicates the unit's operational status to manage and track a unit readiness.
    - **Individuals Awaiting Placement (IAP)**: embers who are currently not assigned to any specific unit. Managing IAPs is critical to ensuring all members are gainfully employed.
    - **Assignment and Chain of Command:**
        - Reserve Sailors are managed by NRC for administrative purposes and by Reserve units operationally.
        - NRA provides administrative support and processes pay and personnel transactions.
        - **Unit Identification Codes (UIC)**:
            - **RUIC (Reserve Unit Identification Code)** identifies a reserve unit.
            - **TRUIC (Training Reserve Unit Identification Code)** identifies an administrative reserve unit responsible for administrative support.
            - **UMUIC (Unit Mobilization Unit Identification Code)** identifies an operational reserve unit responsible for mobilization assignments and warfighting readiness.
            - **AUIC (Active Unit Identification Code)** is used your active unit for mob-to-billet.
- **Locally Assigned** means UMUIC and TRUIC are the same.
- **Non-Locally Assigned**: Refers to being operationally assigned to units other than their admin or "home" units:
    - **Non-Locally Assigned In**: Operational unit for training, mobilization, and warfighting readiness.
    - **Non-Locally Assigned Out**: Administrative unit at their home location that includes pay and records.
    - Coordination between administrative and operational NRAs ensures effective personnel management and readiness.

**Disaster Preparation and NFAAS:**

- Disaster preparation is essential for safety and continuity of operations.
- NFAAS helps account for Navy personnel and their families after disasters.
- Conducting a unit recall and ensuring NFAAS accountability involves contacting members, verifying their status, and instructing them to report in NFAAS.

# Operations Officer

**Reserve Program Director (RPD) or Operations Support Officer (OSO):**

- Facilitate and coordinate operational support to ensure unit readiness and effectiveness.
- Manage training programs, personnel administration, logistics, and administrative tasks.
- Budget Input to CNRFC: Provide data on unit needs for budget allocation.
- Pillars: Support Navy Reserve mission pillars - readiness, integration, support, and service.
- **Operational Support Plan (OSPLAN)**: Outlines specific support requirements for Reserve units.
- **Operational Support Planning and Execution System (OSPADES)**: tool used for planning and managing Reserve support to active-duty commands.

**Best Source of Drills/Orders:**

- **Annual Training (AT)**: Used for mob-to-billet, collective training, and supporting real-world operations, typically for 12-14 days.
- **Active Duty for Training (ADT)**: Often used for training, such as attending a school or course.
- **Inactive Duty Training (IDT)**: Best for routine CONUS missions, training, or tasks that can be accomplished in a 4-hour drill period or over a drill weekend.
- **Inactive Duty Training Travel (IDTT)**: Suitable for CONUS missions where travel is necessary, and the mission can be completed in a short timeframe.
- **Additional Training Periods (ATP)**: Effective for additional training needs beyond regular drill weekends, especially for complex CONUS missions or projects.
- **Readiness Management Periods (RMP)**: Mostly used for administrative work or command readiness activities that don't directly contribute to a specific mission but are essential to overall readiness.
- **NROWS Functions and CO Permissions:**
    - NROWS: Create, route, approve Navy Reserve orders.
    - CO can initiate, review, approve orders, delegate approval authority, and ensure compliance.
- **DTS and Orders Application Process:**
    - DTS: Integrated travel management system for DoD.
    - Process includes authorization creation, travel booking, review, approval, and expense reporting.
- **Proper Use and Restrictions of GTCC:**
    - Use for official travel expenses, emergency expenses.
    - Restrictions include no personal use, cash advances within limits, timely payment.
- **OPSEC Implications of Posting on Social Media:**
    - Risk of compromised missions, personnel safety, reduced trust, and damage to reputation.
    - Mitigation through training, limited sharing of sensitive information, and privacy settings.
- **Access to NRC SharePoint and NRAU SharePoint:**
    - NRC SharePoint: Hub for communication and collaboration for NRCs.
    - NRAU SharePoint: Unit-specific platform for communication and collaboration.
- **Implementation of FY Force Execution Guidance:**
    - Align unit operations with strategic priorities.
    - Manage various reserve funds within policy restrictions.
    - Balancing AT funds with gaining command's requirements is critical.
- **Determining AT Timing and Resource Management:**
    - Strategic planning and collaboration with gaining command.
    - Regular reviews of unit members' skills, qualifications, and availability.
    - Prioritization of mission requirements based on criticality and available resources.
- **Communication with Gaining Command:**
    - Open and transparent communication about funding limitations.
    - Setting clear expectations for Reserve support based on available resources.
    - Helping the gaining command prioritize requirements based on realistic support capabilities.

# Training Officer

**Training Documentation**

- **Annual Unit Training Plan:** outlines the scheduled training activities for a military unit over a specific period, usually one fiscal year.
- **Long-Range Training Plan:** Provides a broader outlook on the unit's training needs, allowing for more comprehensive planning and resource allocation.
- **Planning Board for Training (PB4T)** - a systematic approach to planning and executing unit training:
    - **Planning**: Identify unit training needs, setting objectives, and outlining a schedule.
    - **Briefing**: Communicate objectives, timeline, responsibilities, and expectations to all parties.
    - **Execution**: Ensure resources are available and the schedule is followed, while also being flexible.
    - **Debriefing**: Assess training effectiveness, get feedback, and identify areas for improvement.
- **Naval Officer Billet Classification (NOBC)**: Classify an officer billet with an assigned an NOBC code that represents the primary duties and responsibilities of that position.
- **Additional Qualification Designators (AQD)**: Denotes special skills or qualifications that an officer may earn beyond their primary specialty. This includes language skills, weapons qualifications, or specialized training.

**Active-Duty Recall**

- **Mobilizations**:
    - The Reserve Force is adopting a Billet Based Activation (BBA) posture to support the Navy's warfighting capacity.
    - Mobilizations will focus on strategic depth and supporting Individual Augment (IA) missions.
    - Current mobilization requirements are managed by Commander, U.S. Fleet Forces (USFF) Command, with assignments to active or Reserve components.
    - Reserve Sailors can volunteer for IA mobilizations through the ZipServe application.
    - **Adaptive Mobilizations:**
        - Adaptive Mobilization (AM) process improves speed and capacity for large-scale mobilizations.
        - The AM construct includes distributed activation, mobilization, de-mobilization, and de-activation processes.
        - NMPS locations have expanded to support this process more efficiently.
            - **Navy Mobilization Processing Site (NMPS):**
            - NMPS provides administration, training, equipping, and transportation for mobilizing Sailors.
            - Activation processing includes a five-day period at NMPS before follow-on training or deployment.
- **Mobilization selection and notification process:**
    - **Communication**: Clearly discuss the potential mobilization, ensuring understanding of obligations.
    - **Preparation**: Ensure personnel are ready, maintaining updated records and qualifications.
    - **Support**: Guide member and their family through the process, explaining orders and assisting with tasks.
    - **Continuity of Operations**: Plan for operations during absence, redistributing responsibilities if necessary.
- **Mobilization screening process:**
    - **Verification**: Verify all necessary administrative, medical, dental, and training requirements are up to date.
    - **Coordination**: Arrange for necessary medical or dental examinations, scheduling pre-deployment training, and coordinating with higher headquarters and supporting commands.
    - **Support**: Provide support and guidance to the member during the screening process.
- **During the Mobilization:**
    - **Communication**: Maintain regular communication with the mobilized Sailor and their family.
    - **Advocate**: Unit leaders should act as an advocate for the Sailor if any issues arise during mobilization, liaising with relevant parties to resolve the situation.
    - **Command Individual Augmentee Coordinator (CIAC)** serves as the primary point of contact between the Sailor, the Sailor's family, and the command during mobilization.
- **After Completing a Mobilization:**
    - **Debriefing**: Update the Sailor on changes in the unit during their absence.
    - **Health Checks**: Ensure the Sailor completes mandatory post-deployment health evaluations.
    - **Transition Support**: Provide resources for transitioning back to civilian life and career goals.
- **Medical Codes:**
    - **Medical Readiness Reporting (MRR)**: This code implies a medical issue that requires attention.
    - **Line of Duty (LOD)**: This code implies an injury or illness occurred in the line of duty.
    - **Temporarily Not Physically Qualified (TNPQ)**: Shows a temporary medical condition that may limit a Sailor's ability to perform certain duties or deploy.
    - **Temporarily Not Dentally Qualified (TNDQ)**: Indicates a temporary dental issue.
- **Psychological Health Outreach Program (PHOP)** is designed to enhance mental health and resilience among Sailors and families. It offers clinical assessments, psychoeducation, case management, and referral services.

**Mobilizations Readiness:**

- **Physical Readiness:**
    - Physical fitness is essential for Reserve Sailor readiness.
    - Use resources like the Navy PFA app and PRIMS to maintain and track fitness.
- **Family Care Plan:**
    - Sailors must ensure dependents are cared for during absences.
    - Plans are required for single parents, dual military couples, and those responsible for elderly or disabled family members.
    - Family care plans should be updated annually and under certain circumstances.
- **Childcare:**
    - Plan and utilize available resources for childcare during active duty.
    - Update your Family Care Plan and childcare priority status regularly.
- **Maternity Leave:**
    - Reserve Component Maternity Leave (RCML) provides benefits for birth and non-birth parents under certain conditions.
    - Eligible RC members can receive 12 weeks of non-chargeable leave for qualifying birth events.
- **Medical Readiness:**
    - Medical readiness is critical for mobilization and warfighting readiness.
    - Sailors must complete annual health assessments and dental exams.
- **Line of Duty Healthcare (LODHC):**
    - LODHC benefits are available for injuries or illnesses incurred during duty status.
    - Eligible Sailors may receive medical care, incapacitation pay, and other benefits.
- **TRICARE:**
    - Reserve Sailors have access to TRICARE Reserve Select and TRICARE Dental.
    - Eligibility and benefits vary based on active-duty status and duration of service.
- **Legal Readiness:**
    - Legal assistance is available for active duty over 30 days or for mobilization preparation.
    - Services include drafting wills, powers of attorney, and assistance with SCRA and USERRA rights.
