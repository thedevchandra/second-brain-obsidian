## **â— Symptoms**

- **Primary**: Heel pain during walking
- **Other**: Pain after rest or while standing
- **Typical pattern**:
    - Pain is **worst in the morning**
    - Lessens with walking, but may return after inactivity
    - Can occur during sleep due to foot positioning

## **ğŸ” Diagnosis**

- Based on symptoms: heel tenderness, pain when stretching the sole

## **ğŸ’Š Treatment**

- **Rest**: Avoid walking during flare-ups
- **Medications**: Anti-inflammatories (e.g., ibuprofen, aspirin) for short-term use
- **Ice therapy**: Several times daily, or after activity
- **Footwear**:
    - Cushioned, supportive shoes
    - Orthotic inserts or heel cups
- **Special support**:
    - Night splints
    - Athletic taping
- **Therapy**:
    - Physical therapy to stretch the fascia and strengthen lower leg muscles
    - Cortisone injections in severe cases
- **Surgery**: Rarely required
