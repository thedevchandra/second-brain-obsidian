- [MyNavy Portal](http://www.mnp.navy.mil): official access point to all Navy administrative websites and applications.
- [**Navy Reserve Homeport**](https://www.mynrh.navy.mil/): Primary platform for Reserve personnel to access key resources and tools.
- [Ready to Serve (R2S)](https://r2s.private.navyreserve.navy.mil/) – mobile application offering access to various Navy Reserve tools to enable SELRES to report for duty across the globe and return to the home of record, receive broadcasts, access pay, and personnel information, and send/receive official e-mail.

Records Management:

- [**Navy Standard Integrated Personnel System (NSIPS)**](https://www.nsips.cloud.navy.mil/): The primary HR system for the Navy. As CO, you'd oversee personnel data and administrative actions.
    - ([NSIPS STATUS](https://my.navy.mil/index.html)) Help Desk: 833-637-3669 or [nesd@nesd-mail.onbmc.mil](mailto:nesd@nesd-mai%6C.onbmc.mil) (Email Tips: 1. Use your .MIL address 2. In the Subject Line provide System/Application) DWP: [https://nesd-dwp.onbmc.mil](https://nesd-dwp.onbmc.mil/)
    - Enhanced Drill Management (EDM) - module in NSIPS used to automate Navy Reserve drill management system allowing units to conduct electronic musters and Sailors to reschedule and request additional drills.
    - Electronic Service Record: Regularly review and update your ESR to ensure accuracy. Upon reenlistment, documents from ESR are submitted to your OMPF, which is used by selection boards.
- [Bupers Online](https://www.bol.navy.mil/) (BOL):
    - Advancements/Selection Boards - provides results of advancement boards
    - ARPR/ASOSH Online = Annual Retirement Point Record / Annual Statement of Service History – Retirement points electronically submitted reflect online approximately 60 days from the date of transmission and the anniversary month updates/closes two months after the month passes. It will be reflected online on the first Monday of the third month.
    - CO/XO/CMC Advancement/Selection Board Verification
    - CWAY - Sailor Self-Service - a workflow database application that provides rating quality and eligibility screen for all enlisted Sailors entering the Navy or desiring to reenlist or convert before their 14th year of service. It also identifies rating opportunities for undesignated (PACT) Sailors based on rating needs and Sailor desires.
    - Individual Medical Readiness (IMR) Status - displays medical info such as the Physical Health Assessments (PHA), laboratory results, immunizations, dental readiness, and any medical conditions that may limit deployment and post-deployment health assessments.
    - [Naval Register](https://www.bol.navy.mil/) – the register Number assigned indicates the officer’s relative seniority on the Active-Duty Lists (lineal number) or on the Reserve Active Status List (precedence)
    - Navy Personnel Command Document Services
        - eNavFit Program - a performance evaluation application that helps users create, edit, and route fitness or evaluation reports in addition to providing the ability to digitally sign, validate reports, and automate submissions to PERS-32 for entry into OMPF.
        - Name Change form - NAVPERS 1070/884
        - Navy Department Awards Web Service (NDAWS):
            - Start new OPNAV 1650/3 to recommend personal award
            - Start new OPNAV 1650/14 to recommend unit award
            - Search for awards
        - ESSBD (Submit letter to SelBoard) - [Reserve Officer Promotion Selection Boards](https://www.mynavyhr.navy.mil/Career-Management/Boards/Reserve-Officer/)
            - [In Zone Determination](https://web.archive.org/web/20180820085010/https://www.public.navy.mil/bupers-npc/boards/generalboardinfo/Documents/HowCanITellIfIAmGoingToBeIn.doc) – A NAVADMIN message setting the “zones” is released “usually” in the middle of Dec.
    - Navy-Marine Corps Mobilization Processing System (NMCMPS) - view Mobilization, IA, or ADOS orders
    - ODC, OSR, PSR, ESR
        - **Officer Summary Record (OSR)**: Provides a snapshot of an officer's career.
        - **Performance Summary Record (PSR)**: Summarizes a sailor's performance data.
    - Official Military Personnel File (OMPF) - provides a secure and efficient method for you to access and review your image record 24/7 and is the preferred method of obtaining OMPF information and correcting your records
    - [PRIMS](https://www.mnp.navy.mil/group/performance/prims?utm_source=mnp%20public) or [PRIMS-2](https://navynpc.my.salesforce.mil/) ([Low Bandwith PRIMS](https://www.mnp.navy.mil/group/performance/prims/?lowbandwidth=true?utm_source=mnp%20public)) (Physical Readiness Information Management System Two) – A database for recording and tracking physical readiness information on Active and Reserve Component Navy Service members. (Help Desk: 901-874-2210 or [prims@navy.mil](mailto:pr%69%6Ds@%6Eavy.mil))
    - Selective Reenlistment Bonus
- Defense Finance and Accounting Service ([DFAS](https://www.dfas.mil/)) - LES - a document is given monthly to members of the United States military, which documents their pay and leave status every month.

Apply for Assignments:

- Reserve Force Manpower Tools (RFMT) - a system for NR billet assignments:
    - **APPLY**: A module within Navy Reserve Homeport that allows reservists to apply for billets.
    - **JO APPLY**: Similar to RMFT/APPLY but specifically for junior officers.
- [MyNavy Assignment (MNA)](https://mynavyassignment.dc3n.navy.mil/) - allows Sailors or Command Career Counselors (CCC) to view available jobs:
    - Oversee the detailing process for unit personnel, ensuring they have the necessary skills.
    - Recommending changes to assignments based on mission needs and personnel goals.
    - Ensuring personnel understand how to use it to view and apply for available billets.
- [ZipServe](https://locker.private.navyreserve.navy.mil/zipserve/opportunities) ([ZipServe_Help@navy.mil](mailto:%5AipSer%76e_H%65lp@navy.%6Dil)) = “one-stop-shop” for Reservists to view and apply for volunteer opportunities including Mobilizations, AT, ADT, ADOS, and DR.

Readiness:

- [**Navy Reserve Readiness Module (NRRM)**](https://nrrm.nrre.navy.mil/): A comprehensive readiness tracking tool. (Helpdesk: [NRRM-Support@innovasi.com](mailto:NRRM-Support@inn%6Fvasi%2Ecom) or 619-647-3177)
- **Reserve Unit SharePoint** – Means for organizing the unit documents in an accessible location for all unit members.  Key aspect is to make sure that items that contain PII are only visible to those personnel with the specific need to view those documents.
- [T-Ready](https://bridge.nrdw.navyreserve.navy.mil/tready/#/training-readiness) ([2nd link](https://bridge.nrdw.navyreserve.navy.mil/bridgereservist/#/landing)) - tracks individual Sailor training requirements and related readiness items for use and assessment by command leadership (RPDs and UMUIC COs). Documents include: Personnel Qualification Standards (PQS), Job Qualification Requirements (JQR), On-the-Job Training (OJT), certifications, licenses and qualifications
- [Enterprise Safety Application Management System](https://esams.cnic.navy.mil/esams_gen_2/loginesams.aspx) (ESAMS) = the management center for Navy safety and health training readiness and programs. (Help Desk at 1-865-693-0048)
- **Operational Support Plan Additional Drill Execution Spreadsheet (**[OSPADES](https://locker.private.navyreserve.navy.mil/OSPADES/)) – provides a means for managing the total requirements that will be executed by the RPD to support the mission. All the Operations Officers submit their requirements to the RPD who uses OSPADES to track and disperse funding to the various unit:
    - Unit COs will not authorize additional drills to be scheduled if those drills are not distributed to them.

Training:

- [Fleet Training, Management and Planning System](https://ntmpsweb.dc3n.navy.mil/Fltmps/) (FLTMPS) - access many training, manpower, and personnel reports currently available. Training Officers view and document personnel and training information, view training requirements, and generate training management reports.
- [Electronic Training Jacket (ETJ)](https://ntmpsweb.dc3n.navy.mil/etjclient/DoDBanner.aspx) - View personnel data including training, education, NECs, certifications, qualifications, awards, and career history.
- [Navy eLearning (NeL)](https://learning.nel.navy.mil/ELIAASv2p/) [(NeL2)](https://learning.nel.navy.mil/) – Use to enroll in and complete Navy Schoolhouse, Professional Military Education (PMW), [General Military Training (GMT)](https://www.kellybeamsley.com/navy/#GMT), and Information Assurance courses. ([Help Desk](https://nesd-dwp.onbmc.mil/): 1-833-637-3669, [Chat](https://nesd-dwp.onbmc.mil/), or [need@nesd-mail.onbmc.mil](mailto:n%65e%64%40nesd%2Dmai%6C.%6F%6Ebmc.%6Dil)
- Total Workforce Management System (TWMS) - [MyTWMS,](https://mytwms.dc3n.navy.mil/) [TWMS](https://twms.dc3n.navy.mil/), or [TOPS](https://twms.dc3n.navy.mil/TOPS/) - A database used for civilians AND all Navy Cybersecurity personnel to track training and certifications for Info Assurance. Help Desk: [eas@ctirms.com](mailto:%65%61s%40cti%72%6Ds.c%6Fm) (primary), [TWMSAdministrator@navy.mil](mailto:TWMSAdmi%6Eistra%74o%72@na%76y%2Emil) (secondary); or NMCI Help Desk toll 1-866-843-6624 and select the TWMS option.
- [enterprise Navy Training Reservation System](https://app.prod.cetars.training.navy.mil/eNTRS/) (eNTRS) – provides the means for Fleet activities to view and request seats in designated Navy schools. It is the primary system for checking/acting on seats for CANTRAC courses.
- [Catalog of Navy Training Courses (CANTRAC)](https://app.prod.cetars.training.navy.mil/cantrac/vol2.html) – a NAVEDTRA publication, which contains information concerning all formal Courses of instruction throughout the Navy
- CNRFC N7 Schools page – CNRFC ADT Schools Home Page under N71, contains links to assorted Navy School Sites

Family and Dependents:

- [**Navy Family Accountability and Assessment System (NFAAS)**](https://navyfamily.navy.mil/) is a tool used to account for Navy personnel and their families during significant events, such as natural disasters or emergencies.
- Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System (DEERS) lists those who are eligible for military benefits, including service members, DoD civilian employees, and their dependents.
