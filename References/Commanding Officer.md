**Fundamentals:**

- Responsibility for the unit's mission, personnel, and resources.
- Accountability involves answering for the outcomes of decisions and actions.
- Authority empowers the CO to make decisions and enforce standards within the command.
- "Charge of Command" emphasizes the CO's responsibility, accountability, and authority in leading a command effectively.

**Command Philosophy, Ethics, and Leadership:**

- "Charge of Command" outlines three essential principles: mission accomplishment, caring for people, and leading by example.
- Trust is paramount, both among subordinates and with higher-level commanders.
- Navy standards for personal conduct are influenced by the Navy's history and values.
- The CO's leadership style is self-assessed, with strengths and weaknesses.
- Work-life balance is crucial, considering various personal responsibilities of unit members.
- Encouraging coordination with unit leadership and managing tasks to accommodate changes in responsibilities is essential.

**Command Relationships and Trust:**

- Command Triad (CO, XO, SEL) works together to create a safe and effective work environment.
- Utilizing the XO and SEL involves fostering their leadership roles and building a strong working relationship.

**Command Climate, Recruiting, and Retention:**

- Command climate influences morale and mission effectiveness.
- COs shape command climate through leadership actions, including fostering inclusiveness and mutual respect.
- Recognizing signs of morale decline and addressing them proactively is crucial.

**Sailor and Family Support (301.33, 301.34, 301.35):**

- Family readiness impacts mission readiness, with support programs available for Sailors and families.
- The Command Ombudsman plays a vital role in supporting families.
- Addressing sensitive issues like domestic violence, sexual assault, and substance abuse requires promoting a climate where Sailors feel comfortable seeking help.

**Commanding Officer’s Safety Responsibilities (301.37, 301.38):**

- COs create a culture of safety and influence safety practices within the unit.
- Empowering members to question safety practices and establishing clear safety protocols are essential.

**Navy Mission and Leadership Competencies (301.39, 301.40, 301.41, 301.36):**

- Incorporating strategic-level instructions into everyday actions reinforces the Navy's mission.
- Instilling Navy pride and professionalism draws from the Navy's heritage and history.
- Understanding the Navy's mission guides the CO's role in command leadership and support.

**Gaining Command Mission and Relationships (301.42, 301.43, 301.44):**

- Understanding gaining command requirements and communicating effectively with the ISIC are essential.
- Command briefs and supporting the command's mission demonstrate readiness for transition.

**Commanding Officer’s Programmatic Responsibilities (301.45, 301.46):**

- By-Direction Authority and the unit muster process highlight administrative responsibilities.
- Ensuring accurate records and adhering to policies maintain command integrity.

**Performance Evaluation and Feedback (301.47, 301.48, 301.49, 301.50):**

- Managing the Performance Evaluation System involves drafting evaluations, conducting mid-term counseling, and providing feedback.
- Mid-term counseling and annual performance evaluations serve different purposes, with specific emphasis on performance expectations.
- Managing CAI populations requires coordination and tracking of training requirements.

**Personnel / Performance Recognition (301.51, 301.52):**

- Recognition programs boost morale and contribute to recruiting and retention efforts.
- Awards impact unit morale positively and can incentivize performance and retention efforts.
