**Drill Pay: Navy Reserve Sailors receive pay allowances for their part-time service:**

- Based on active duty pay and allowances.
- Drill pay is 1/30th of basic pay for grade and years of service.
- Same pay and benefits as active-duty sailors when on active duty orders.

**Different types of drill periods:**

- **Inactive Duty Training (IDT):**
    - Monthly drills performed on IDT weekends.
    - One paid period is four hours, one non-paid period is two hours.
    - Four IDT periods per typical weekend, totaling four days of basic pay.
    - Can be rescheduled for conflicts or needs of the Navy.
    - Completion of 40 of 48 drill periods for a satisfactory Reserve participation year.
- **Inactive Duty Training - Travel (IDTT):**
    - Similar to IDT but includes authorized funding for travel.
    - Allows non-local Reserve members to attend drills at alternate sites.
- **Additional Flight Training Periods (AFTP):**
    - For aviation personnel, focused solely on flight duties.
    - Not distributed outside aviation commands.
- **Additional Training Periods (ATP):**
    - Additional paid IDT periods for specific units to support mobilization missions.
    - Discretionary, allowing Reserve Program Directors to provide additional support.
- **Readiness Management Periods (RMP):**
    - Additional paid IDT supporting day-to-day unit operations such as administration, training preparation, and maintenance.
    - Only one RMP credited per day, cannot be performed on the same day as other IDT.
    

**Inactive Duty for Training - Reimbursable (IDT-R)**

- Aimed at enhancing operational unit readiness by providing reimbursement for travel expenses from residence to the assigned drill location.
- Cover up to $500 per drill weekend (12/year) for transportation, lodging (excluding provided lodging), and meals.
- Eligibility is outlined in [COMNAVRESFORNOTE 1570](https://www.navyreserve.navy.mil/Portals/35/CNRFNOTE%201570_25Oct2022.pdf?ver=jyJikPVvQ3gwrs1M_ImjCg%3d%3d) and depends on factors like:
    - Residing 150 miles or more from their billet assignment,
    - Holding qualifying ratings or leadership positions, and
    - Being associated with an eligible billet.
- Separate funding from IDTT, with IDT-R catering to travel from home to the assigned location.

**Orders in the Navy Reserve encompass various types of duty:**

- **Annual Training (AT) / Active Duty Training (ADT):**
    - Managed through the Navy Reserve Order Writing System (NROWS).
    - AT: Selected Reserve personnel required to complete 12-14 days annually as scheduled.
    - ADT: Intended to enhance or refresh skills supporting military operations.
- **Active Duty Operational Support (ADOS):**
    - Supports Navy mission requirements without programmed permanent duty positions.
    - Orders issued for 30 days or more, not exceeding 365 days and not crossing fiscal years.
- **Definite and Indefinite Recall:**
    - Definite recall for SELRES personnel is voluntary and temporary, typically ranging from 12 to 36 months.
    - Indefinite recall for Reserve officers is voluntary, permanent, offering a pathway to a career change to active-duty service.

**Reserve Pay Assistance:**

- Resolve pay issues with unit leadership, Navy Reserve Center (NRC), or MNCC.

**GTCC (Government Travel Charge Card):**

- Primary payment method for official government travel.
- Follow guidelines to avoid misuse and disciplinary actions.
