## 7 Steps to Creating an Online Course

**Presenter: Ankur Nagpal & Jess Catorc**: [https://teachable.com/home-summit](https://teachable.com/home-summit)

Time: Tuesday, March 24 @ 11:00 am ET

### Why Online Courses are Amazing

1. Scale
2. Money
3. Faster Results
4. Room to Breathe

### 1 - Find your most profitable course idea (3-Step Process):

1. Brainstorm topics you’re passionate about, secondary skills you have, content your fans ask for all the time
2. Determine the transformation you’re going to provide for your students
3. Choose the most impactful transformation and create your course

### 2 - Set your income goals for your course using our formula

- Revenue Goal = (Total Followers x Conversion Rate) x Course Price
- 2% audience conversion rule

### 3 - Grow your course audience and email list with our playbook - give away value for email:

- Giveaways - free content
    - King Sumo: [https://kingsumo.com/](https://kingsumo.com/)
    - Gleam: [https://gleam.io/app/competitions](https://gleam.io/app/competitions)
- Mini-Course - giveaway small section of your course
- Webinar - go live and build a personal connection with the audience
- Coaching/Strategy Session

### 4 - Price your course the right way

- Double the price you feel comfortable with
- Attract more engage/prepared students

### 5 - Create your school and a high-converting course sales page - 6 components:

1. Title and Description
2. Subtitle
3. Promo Video
4. Course Description
5. Testimonials
6. Payment Plan

### 6 - Create and record a course people love

- Outline Course - step-by-step with the desired outcome
- Create Sections - keep it to 6-12 sections with video lectures
- Video Framework - what they are about to learn; why is it important; how to do it
- Most important thing is providing transformation
- How do you create course content?
    - Screen Flow: [https://screenflow.en.softonic.com/mac](https://screenflow.en.softonic.com/mac)
    - Camtasia: [https://www.techsmith.com/video-editor.html](https://www.techsmith.com/video-editor.html)
- Some further content ideas: interviews with experts; cheatsheets; case studies; supporting materials; toolkits

### 7 - Launch your course using our course marketing plan

- Course Launch - when you open enrollment for a fixed time period and close enrollment to focus on your students
- 2-phase launch over the 30-day period
    - Phase 1: Educate - provide value, generate demand, and gain trust
    - Phase 2: Launch - small list with a few hundred people; exact calendar:
        1. Pre-launch: Course teaser, mention you’ll be launching a course
        2. Pre-launch: Course description email
        3. Sales: Course is open for enrollment
        4. Sales: “Is this course right for me?” + FAQs
        5. Sales: Surprise bonus added to the course offer
        6. Sales: Thank you and social proof
        7. Sales: Logical argument for why your course makes sense
        8. Sales: Enrollment is closing (Can send and extra 1-2 emails)

## 5 Ways to Grow Your Email List and Turn Subscribers into Customers

**Presenter: Melyssa Griffin**: [https://www.melyssagriffin.com/](https://www.melyssagriffin.com/)

Time: Tuesday, March 24 @ 2:00 pm ET

### 1 - Discover your List-Building Personality Type

List-Building Personality Type - method that is aligned with your skills, personality, and purpose; typically have primary and secondary

- Passionate Performer (11%)
    - Extremely outgoing; thrive when performing; charismatic; extroverted
    - Entrepreneurial Path: be a speaker at events; host workshops or retreats; teach webinars or courses
    - Struggle: converting people; difficulty with details
- Vibrant Visionary (13%)
    - Trailblazer (do things differently); innovative; creative
    - Entrepreneurial Path: an entrepreneurial path with creativity and variety
    - Struggle: monotonous; fall trap of what they should do
- **Charming Collaborator (18%)**
    - Enjoy life with people and meeting others; community over collaboration
    - Entrepreneurial Path: building community; network/collaborate with others
    - Struggle: trying to create their business on own
- Friendly Expert (23%)
    - Natural teachers; empathetic; kind-hearted; love to learn to help others
    - Entrepreneurial: using knowledge to help others through courses, consulting, teaching workshops, or events
    - Struggle: giving too much for free
- Quiet Genious (35%)
    - Not-necessarily quiet; work behind-the-scenes; love spreadsheets; contemplative; strategists
    - Entrepreneurial Path: find ways to support people; iterate on business ideas over time; see a solution that no one else can
    - Struggle: think they need to be on video and collaborating with others

### 2- How should you grow your email list based on your Type?

- Passionate Performer
    - Video training and lead magnets; host joint webinars to teach a class
    - Use Instagram stories, YouTube, and Facebook Live
    - Speak at local events
- Vibrant Visionary
    - Research the most popular list-building methods and put a unique spin
    - Add creativity into your ideas and do something no one else has
- **Charming Collaborator**
    - Focus efforts on your community like having a free Facebook group
    - Reach out to people in your industry to collaborate with them: podcasts, webinar, Facebook live in a group, free challenge, social media takeover
    - Collaborate with others and don’t go at it alone
- Friendly Expert
    - Excellent teacher
    - Create free worksheets, training, challenges, or PDFs
    - Create a free Facebook group just for subscribers: host Q&A sessions
- Quiet Genius
    - Analyze list-building methods to make them better
    - Split-test landing pages, test different PDF lead magnets, or experiment
    - Well-researched content and going deep on list-building methods

### 3 - Key things to keeping in mind

- Be a Problem Solver - build trust with your content/freebies to build your list
- People use the internet to solve problems: Google, Pinterest, YouTube
- Where are your people hanging out? Facebook groups; Instagram; conference

### 4 - Why is now the best time?

- Social media engagement rate: 0.58% versus Email open rate: 22.86%
- Email Marketing: 40X more effective than social media
- Buying Process: 3X more via email than social media
- Number of Email Users: Grow the 3 Billion people in 2020
- Over 91% of people access their email account at least once per day

### 5 - Proven Strategy for Engagement and Conversions:

- Consumers are likely to classify emails as spam if they don’t like them
- Consumers are getting more emails overall, so will tune out if they lack value
- Best results using strengths and List-Building Personality Type
- There’s no one way to build an email list

## How to Connect with Your Clients—and Gain New Ones—Through the Power of Email

**Presenter: Laura Belgray**: [https://talkingshrimp.com/](https://talkingshrimp.com/)

Time: Wednesday, March 25 @ 2:00 pm ET

### Why is Email so Powerful?

- 58% of adults check email in bed (getting up, going to bed)
- 42% of adults check email on the toilet
- It’s fair, intimate, gratifying

### EFAB: Email for a Bro/Bestie/Boo

- Show the real you
    - Can’t control what people think of you - only what they see of you
    - Intimate, causal, vulnerable - embarrassing stories
    - Flawsome - connect with failures and flaws
- How would you talk to a friend?
    - Conversational
    - Write to One person NOT to everyone
    - Informal (but intriguing) subject line
- Subject Lines: [https://talkingshrimp.com/subjectlines](https://talkingshrimp.com/subjectlines)
    - Don’t want to be all business-y
        - This Problem Is Keeping Me Up At Night
        - *This is keeping me up at night*
    - Don’t give away the whole story
        - Why bananas have the most potassium.
        - *What’s cheap, delicious, and stops leg cramps?*
    - Play with punctuation - know your audience
        - This is so you.
        - *This is so you*
    - Source personal subject lines from you inbox from the early 2000s
        - Writing gig; dinner dates; Meeting; fact; “Hee hee I’m so mean!,” i want to be more like yous two
    - Don’t make the subject line and the preview text the same
        - Options:
            - Active Campaign: [https://www.activecampaign.com/](https://www.activecampaign.com/)
            - Convert Kit (html): [https://convertkit.com/](https://convertkit.com/)
        - Don’t leave the preview text in “newsletter mode”
        - Do send yourself a text before you send to see if you want to open your own email

### Get Regular - consistency

- It builds trust - buy from people we know, like, and trust
- Don’t be the drunk uncle - don’t show up out of the blue
- What to do if you’ve ghosted your list
    - Email them now
    - Slide back in and pretend like nothing happened
    - People are busy, didn’t notice, and may think it’s their fault
- It pays to be prolific - email more with different themes for specific days
    - When 3x email rate it lead to 2x more sales
    - Each email requires less work
- Have a welcome sequence - treats all subscribers with greeting
    - Use engagement numbers to modify welcome sequence
    - Doesn’t have to be epic or perfect
    - No one ever unsubscribed because your email was too short
    - You are not bugging them with repetitiveness or recycling content
- Don’t get caught in the Value Trap
    - Value = make me feel something
    - Value = make me see something differently
    - Value = recommend one thing you like
    - Value = inspire me to try something new
    - Value = give me a break from what I (should be) working on
    - Value = make me feel less of a weirdo/freak/loser/failure
    - “I Sooooo Needed to Read this Today”

### 3 - Tell stories that tie in

- Even if it’s not part of the main message
- Remind them what you offer and ways to take action - put it in the PS

## The Customer Map Workshop

**Presenter: Nicole Walters:** [https://nicolewalters.com/](https://nicolewalters.com/)

Time: Thursday, March 26 @ 8:00 pm ET

### Customer - Relationship Management Cycle

- Exact Method - grow business with zero dollars and debt free
- People / Payment = Growth: Strong Customer Relationships Yield Strong Profits
- How do I get paying customers? Customer - Relationship Management Cycle
- When it comes to your face. Say no to walk-ins.

### Marketing - Client Attraction

- Client Attraction - Leverages marketing to connect, engage, and build a relationship with your prospective customers
- Elements
    - Attract ideal target audiences
    - Engage to start a relationship
    - 5-7 interactions for prospect to build know, like, and trust
    - Prepare them to purchase products
- Best Practices
    - Create intentional content for each avatar
    - Align content with Intro and Main products
    - Speak to the problem and the different beliefs that may be preventing them from moving forward
    - Uses stories to show you understand
    - Have a clear Call to Action (CTA)
- Client Attraction Plan - “If people can’t find you, they can’t pay you”
    - Content
        - Podcast
        - Live Broadcasts
        - Social Media Posts
        - Events
        - Collaborations
    - Email List Opt-Ins
        - Fierce Clarity Course
        - Launch Activities
    - Email List Warm-up
        - Email touchpoints during non-launch
        - Increased emails during launch
    - Call to Action
        - Fierce Clarity Course
        - $1K 1Day Course
        - Other products

### Sales - Client Acquisition

- Client Acquisition - utilizes the sales process to create a conversation that will ultimate that will ultimately your customer make the best decision to move forward
- Elements
    - Make an offer
    - Engage in sales conversation
    - Address objections
    - Transfer belief
- Ethical Sales - dignified conversation between two people that opens the possibility for the prospect to act and move from where they are to the next step of a transformation
    - Connecting with your prospect in an authentic way
    - Helping prospect feel heard and respected
    - Matching them with the best offer that fits their current need
    - Clarify what they need to consider to make a decision
    - Supporting them to move into action for what is for them
- Sales Objection - question, concern, fear, or obstacle that prevents them from deciding
    - Identify what holds people back from making a decision
    - Sales Objection Tools - help prospect move through those obstacles in order to make a yes/no decision
        - Say, “Tell me more about that”
        - Ask, “What do you mean by…{initial objection}”
            - I know how you feel…
            - I felt the same way…
            - Here’s what I found…
    - Common Sales Objections
        - It’s too expensive; I just don’t have the money for it
        - Just give me some info and I’ll think about it
        - I need to ask my spouse; I’ve already spent so much money
        - How do I know this will really work?
    - Overcome Sales Objections
        - Probe to see if they want the transformation
        - Find out what is the specific obstacle
        - Guide them to identify what that obstacle is costing them
        - Help them see that the first step is the hardest, but you are wil them

### Customer Service - Client Retention

- Client Retention - providing great customer service that welcomes your customers and helps them achieve the promised transformation
- Elements
    - Welcome customers
    - Deliver what was promised
    - Help them achieve results
    - Manage complaints
- Best Practices
    - Welcome customers though onboarding
    - Set the right expectations - syllabus, schedule (structure)
    - Maintain a conversation - email and call
    - Anticipate the customer needs
    - Deliver on what you promise
    - Celebrate wins
    - Ask for feedback - pre-survey, post-survey
- How to Manage Complaints
    - Get the full story
    - Identify the true problem
    - Identify the resolution the client is asking for
    - Propose a resolution or asset the final decision
