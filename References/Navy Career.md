**Enlisted Academics:**

- **PMK-EE:** The Professional Military Knowledge Eligibility Exam (PMK-EE) is required for Sailors to advance to the next paygrade.
    - It is accessible via MyNavy Portal, Navy e-Learning, and a mobile app.
    - Sailors must complete PMK-EE by the first day of the month when the Navy Wide Advancement Exam (NWAE) is administered.
- **Enlisted Leader Development:**
    - Courses focus on character, ethics, self-awareness, decision-making, and leadership.
    - Courses include:
        - Foundational Leader Development Course: E-3 & E-4 (3 days)
        - Intermediate Leader Development Course: E-5 (3 days)
        - Advanced Leader Development Course: E-6 (4 days)
        - Chief Petty Officer Leader Development: E-7 (5 days)
    - Graduation from these courses will be required for advancement to E-6, E-7, and E-8 starting in 2025.
- **Enlisted Assignments:**
    - Managed via MyNavy Assignment (MNA), a web-based application for researching and applying for jobs.
    - The MNA Reserve schedule follows a quarterly cycle with application, command, and selection phases.
    - Sailors can apply for up to seven billets per cycle.
- Reserve enlisted assignments: RESPERSMAN M-1001.5 (CH-13), Articles 1000-1300]

**Officer Assignments:**

- Officers use JO APPLY for career opportunities, with a similar phased process as enlisted assignments.
    - Application: Potential candidates apply for billets.
    - Ranking: Ranks command Reserve Program Directors and adds feedback for candidates.
    - Selection/directed: CNRFC reviews all applicants, rankings and comments and selects officers for billets and direct assignments.
- APPLY is used for CO/OIC billets:
    - Record Review: Allows potential candidates time to review personal records before applying for a billet. Letters to the board may be submitted to list any missing application information or comments.
    - Application: Registration and dream sheet, is where potential applicants make a list of billets to be considered for.
    - Ranking: APPLY board reviews and briefs records to the panel. Here, board members vote to determine confidence factors.
    - Slating: Applicants come before the board to slate for a billet, which is based on confidence factor and top choice of remaining billets.

**Career Waypoints (C-WAY):**

- A force management tool balancing manning across rates and components.
- Allows for rating conversion opportunities and reenlistment requests.
- **Reserve/Active/TAR Conversions:** Programs like Definite Recall, Indefinite Recall, and SELRES to TAR support transitions between components.

**Learning and Development Roadmaps (LaDRs):**

- Provide rate-specific guidance for career development.
- Updated annually to maintain relevance.

**Retirement:**

- Eligibility generally requires 20 Qualifying Years of service.
- Frequent review of the Statement of Service is essential.
- Retirement requests and processes vary based on whether retiring with or without pay.

**Thrift Savings Plan (TSP):**

- TSP offers retirement benefits with low expenses, multiple investment options, and loan/withdrawal capabilities.

**High Year Tenure (HYT) and Volunteer Training Unit (VTU):**

- **High Year Tenure (HYT):**
    - HYT limits service years based on rank and includes all service in Active, Reserve, and inactive components.
    - It serves as a management tool for shaping the Navy's total force.
    - Exceptions allow Reserve Sailors to extend their careers beyond HYT restrictions.
- **HYT Waivers and VTU:**
    - The Voluntary Training Unit (VTU) is part of the Individual Ready Reserve (IRR) and helps Sailors achieve qualifying years for retirement.
    - VTU members must meet the same requirements as drilling Reserve Sailors but do not receive pay for drills.
    - Sailors can request an HYT waiver to remain in a paid billet or transfer to the VTU to make up for lost years and reach retirement eligibility.
    - E4 to E6 Sailors can transfer to the VTU upon reaching their HYT date, and E7 and above can serve in the VTU until 30 years of service.
- **HYT Plus Pilot Program:**
    - Announced in NAVADMIN 288/22, the HYT Plus Pilot Program suspends HYT for two years to improve retention and readiness.
    - Reserve Sailors can continue service through 30 September 2024, provided they are in good standing and recommended for retention.
    - Sailors must submit a request through their Navy Reserve Center (NRC) to stay under the HYT Plus program.
- **VTU to SELRES:**
    - VTU Sailors in certain rates may apply for paid Reserve billets through an HYT waiver.
    - Requests are submitted through the NRC Command Career Counselor.
- **Career Waypoint:**
    - Offers opportunities for Sailors in overmanned rates or facing advancement challenges to change ratings for continued career progression.
    - Provides monthly updates on rates open for conversion.
    - For more information, Sailors should consult their NRC Command Career Counselor.

- **Correspondence Courses:** Offer retirement points and valuable knowledge for completion.
- **Joint Qualified Officer (JQO):** Officers can attain joint qualifications through duty assignments and joint experiences.
- **Military Education:** Numerous opportunities for professional growth through various military education programs.
- **Navy Credentialing Opportunities On-Line (COOL):** Funds certification and licensing exams mapping military education and training to civilian credentials.
- **United Service Military Apprenticeship Program (USMAP):** Provides certificates of completion for documenting work experience during military service.
- **Academic Degree Record:**
    - Sailors are responsible for submitting official transcripts to the Joint Services Transcript (JST) office.
    - JST translates military experience into civilian terms for academic and employment purposes.
