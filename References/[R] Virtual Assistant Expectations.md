## 1. Availability & Communication

1. **Working Hours**
    - Align primary working hours with the tech founder’s business schedule in San Francisco. Please log all hours with specific tasks in Toggl.
    - Be willing to adjust the schedule for occasional urgent tasks or meetings outside regular hours. If something happens during the workday, either we don’t have to work all hours or work late/weekend.
2. **Responsiveness & Clarity**
    - Respond to What’s App messages (e.g., within a few minutes during active working hours).
    - Communicate updates, blockers, or questions clearly and concisely.
3. **Channels of Communication**
    - Use agreed-upon platforms for daily communication (e.g., What’s App, email, or Notion).
    - Be proactive in suggesting quick calls or video calls to resolve complex issues.

---

## 2. Task Management

1. **Project Tracking & Organization**
    - Use task/project management software (e.g., Notion) to keep track of tasks, deadlines, and progress.
    - Tag tasks with the correct priority, links, and due dates to maintain clarity.
2. **Daily & Weekly Check-ins**
    - Provide a brief summary of completed tasks, ongoing projects, and upcoming deadlines at the end of each day.
    - Future: Share a weekly overview, highlighting achievements, challenges, and next-step priorities.
3. **Time Management & Efficiency**
    - Efficiently organize work time to meet deadlines and handle multiple assignments.
    - Suggest improvements to existing processes to save time and effort.
    - Please log all hours with specific tasks in Toggl.

---

## 3. Technical Competence

1. **Software & Tools Proficiency**
    - Demonstrate proficiency in the required tools and software, such as:
        - Google Workspace (Drive, Docs, Sheets, Slides)
        - Calendar management (Google & Notion Calendar)
        - Project & CRM management apps (Notion)
        - Communication platforms (What’s App, Gmail, Google Meet)
        - AI (Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity)
    - Be open to learning new software or technical tools used by the tech founder.
2. **Research & Data Handling**
    - Use AI (Perplexity) to conduct online research for market trends, competitor analysis, or relevant tech updates as needed.
    - Maintain data accuracy and confidentiality when handling sensitive information.
3. **Tech Troubleshooting**
    - Basic troubleshooting skills for common software or hardware issues. Go to AI or YouTube.
    - Escalate more complex technical problems to me promptly.

---

## 4. Professionalism & Confidentiality

1. **Reliability & Trust**
    - Handle confidential information (e.g., product roadmaps, personal data, financial details) with the utmost discretion.
    - Follow through on commitments and inform me if delays or issues arise.
2. **Cultural Awareness**
    - Be mindful of time-zone differences when I’m traveling.
3. **Quality of Work & Attention to Detail**
    - Submit well-reviewed deliverables, double-checking for accuracy and clarity.
    - Demonstrate professionalism (to slightly informal informal) in written communications and documentation.

---

## 5. Proactive Problem-Solving

1. **Initiative**
    - Identify potential issues or improvements and suggest solutions.
    - Offer ideas to streamline workflow or increase productivity.
2. **Adaptability**
    - Embrace changes in technology, processes, or priorities with minimal disruption.
    - Stay informed about industry trends and relevant skill updates.
3. **Continuous Learning**
    - Pursue ongoing professional development (e.g., online courses, webinars) to enhance skills.
    - Share newfound knowledge that may help the founder or the business.

---

## 6. Relationship Building & Company Culture

1. **Team Collaboration**
    - When interfacing with other teams (e.g., developers, designers, marketing), maintain respectful and clear communication.
    - Recognize that your efficiency and professionalism reflect on me.
2. **Positive Mindset & Support**
    - Uphold a can-do attitude; find ways to help alleviate pressure from the founder’s schedule.
    - Provide candid feedback and insights when needed, always aiming for constructive, solutions-focused discussions.
3. **Representation of the Founder**
    - When communicating on behalf of the tech founder (e.g., drafting emails, booking appointments), embody their style and tone.
    - Maintain a high level of courtesy and professionalism that reflects well on the founder’s personal brand.
